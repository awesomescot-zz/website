Experience

Contractor for GoPro April 2015 - December 2015

DevOps Engineer

GoPro had a team of about six devops engineers when I joined. They were using a combination of Ansible, EC2, Docker, Grafana, and Smart Stack. One of my larger projects was the removal of Smart Stack, which was being used for services discovery, from all of their environments. This was a very sensitive project as Smart Stack was a central part of their infrastructure, I moved them to a much simpler ELB (elastic load balancer) solution. When we rolled this out in production we had absolutly zero issues, so it was a great success. I also helped them automate creating a VPC and infrastructure for a new environment, using Cloud Formation.

DevOps Engineer

I had to pick up the current infrastructure quickly and be able to support it as the only devops engineer within 2 months. Working on cloud instances in RackSpace, I supported a PHP application with Percona Mysql and MongoDB. All server configuration was in Chef. Working with many tools that I had already experienced to monitor servers and manage logs, and adding Sensu, Gluster, and Capistrano. I setup an environment in AWS for them to migrate to, but sadly I did not get to see it completed.

Locus Energy January 2012 - June 2013

Unix Sysadmin

I was hired on as the first sysadmin for Locus Energy. I brought the systems from being in a completely unmanaged and unmonitored state of about 20 servers, to a managed and monitored state of over 80 servers by implementing the following: Hostname/DNS, Puppet, Ganglia, Nagios, backups with Rsync and scripting creation of servers through the ec2 api. I put together an open source consolidated logging system with Rsyslog, Logstash, Elastic Search, Graphite and Graylog2. I also managed some of their existing infrastructure including Mysql, Vertica, Glassfish, Nginx and Haproxy. Since I had no mentor, this job forced me to learn everything from scratch. This job helped me hone my ability to build out, maintain, and monitor Linux systems.

UC Berkeley, Student Housing May 2009 - June 2010

Unix Systems Administrator

I worked with a team of 8 students and 2 experts, to maintain and improve the network that all dorms used. Over 7000 students used our network. Uptime and reliability were very important. I worked with Free-BSD, RHEL, Bash scripting, python, and configuring many services. Also I learned how to manage the physical hardware in a server room. This job gave me a great introduction to unix sysadmining as a whole.
